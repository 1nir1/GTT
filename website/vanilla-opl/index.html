GTT - Graph To Table

Convert hand writing grpah to a real
(X , Y) coordinates

Process - Just 4 easy steps

1) Draw a hand writing graph

The image contains x and y axes, maximum x and y values and a continuous graph line

2) Capture the image with your camera

3) Run GTT

4) Final excel

The final excel contains a table of (x,y) values and a graph

Motivation

JP Morgan developed an automatic robot that analysis the market state (e.g stock price).
The robot gets a simple table of a stock price at a given time interval,
and then tries to give a prediction of the stock price in the future.
Until today, JP Morgan used stock price historical scenarios, in order to train the robot predictions.
With our simple solution, each worker can produce a simple graph and convert it to a valid input for the robot.

Internal self developed algorithms

How is it done?

We run three major operations - detect graph line, detect digits, and detect axes.
Then we pass the output of these into one process that integrates the outputs to a single final template.

Axis detecion

We divide the source image to vertical and horizontal stripe-units, each unit is 5% from the total picture's width/height.
Going over those units - we find the horizontal stripe-unit with the most dots - it will be the x axis.
Then we find the vertical stripe-unit with the most dots - it will be the y axis.
Recognizing the x and y axes, helped us to filter them out of the final output.

Digit recognaizer

We've trained a deep learning nueral network on MNIST dataset.
We find all possible digits in the image, crop them out and then check them with our trained model.
We assmeble all those recognized digits to numbers based on their position and use those numbers to scale the final output.

Graph line

We go over the source image once more and cut off all the irrelevant parts to the graph we've already found (numbers, axes)
On the remaining points (as seen here below) we preform linear-transformation, using the scale we calculated from the extracted numbers.
Those points, after the transformation, are sent to the final output-generating module.
